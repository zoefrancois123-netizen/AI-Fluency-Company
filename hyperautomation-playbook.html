The Hyperautomation Playbook

Where most AI investment goes wrong — and what to fix first.

By Zoe Francois, Founder of The AI Fluency Company

Adding AI to a broken process doesn't fix the process — it accelerates the breakage.

Most organisations investing in AI are making the same mistake: they are automating before they understand what is actually broken. The result is expensive, disappointing, and often leaves the organisation worse off than when they started.

This playbook explains why most AI ROI fails before the AI is even switched on. It is not a technical manual. It is a decision framework for executives who want to invest in AI wisely — and avoid the traps that catch most organisations.

Fix the process first. Then apply AI. The order matters more than the technology.

What's inside.

  • The automation trap — why faster bad is still bad, and how to spot it before you invest.
  • The process audit — a simple framework for deciding what to fix, what to automate, and what to leave alone.
  • The AI readiness test — five questions that reveal whether your organisation is ready for AI, or just ready to waste money.
  • The hyperautomation sequence — the right order: simplify, standardise, automate, then apply intelligence.
  • Common failure patterns — the mistakes every executive team makes, and how to avoid them.
  • The governance checkpoint — how to know when you are ready to scale, and when to pause.

Roughly 20 pages, written for the executive who needs to make the decision — not the engineer who will implement it.

Best read alongside The Executive's Guide to AI Governance and the case study. The playbook tells you what to fix; the guides tell you how to govern it.
